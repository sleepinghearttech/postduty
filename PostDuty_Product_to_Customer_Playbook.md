# PART 1 — Finding products (online + local)

## 1.1 The PostDuty product filter — score before you source
Every candidate product must pass this 7-point filter. Score each 0–2; **only source products scoring ≥11/14.**

| # | Criterion | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Medico-specific (a lay person wouldn't buy it) | Niche defensibility; matches your Instagram audience |
| 2 | **Not a medical device** — accessory/stationery/apparel only | Avoids CDSCO medical-device regulation entirely |
| 3 | Light + small (**under 500 g packed**, ideally under 200 g) | Keeps shipping in the lowest courier slab (~₹26–45) |
| 4 | Unbreakable / no expiry / no sizes | No returns headaches, no dead stock |
| 5 | Landed cost ≤ 35% of selling price (≥65% gross margin) | Low-ticket items must absorb ~₹40–60 shipping + 2% Razorpay + packaging |
| 6 | Personalizable or bundle-able | Name-on-product justifies margin IndiaMART generics can't match; bundles fix the low-AOV problem |
| 7 | Photographs well in 6–8 shots with phone + daylight | Your entire funnel is visual (Instagram) |

**The AOV problem (read this before sourcing anything):** a ₹149 badge reel with ₹45 shipping, ₹8 packaging, and ₹3 Razorpay fee leaves very little even at 65% margin. The fix is not more expensive products — it's **kits and bundles**: "Ward Survival Kit ₹499", "Intern Starter Pack ₹599", "Night Duty Kit ₹449". Design the catalog so the *default* purchase is ₹400–600, with singles available. Free shipping above ₹499 pushes people into bundles.

## 1.3 Where to find them — online
Work through these in order; capture everything in a sourcing sheet (template in 1.5).

1. **IndiaMART (primary wholesale source).** Search the exact product + "wholesale". Filter for suppliers with GST, 3+ years, TrustSEAL if possible. Message 3–5 suppliers per product asking: minimum order quantity (MOQ), price at 20/50/100 pcs, sample cost, dispatch time, customization options. Relevant category pages to start from: retractable badge reels (dir.indiamart.com/impcat/retractable-badge-reel.html), pen torches (dir.indiamart.com/impcat/pen-torches.html). **Always order a paid sample before any bulk order.**
2. **Meesho / Amazon.in / Flipkart bestseller pages** — not to buy, but to validate demand and price ceilings: search each candidate product, note review counts (proxy for volume) and the price band customers already accept.
3. **Etsy India + Instagram competitor stores** — search Instagram for #medicoaccessories #nurseaccessoriesindia #medstudentindia and study the 5–10 Indian pages already selling to this audience: their bestsellers (look at pinned posts / highlights), price points, and gaps. Gaps = your Tier B priorities.
4. **AliExpress/Alibaba trend check only** — see what's trending globally in medical accessories (badge toppers, retractable pen holders, cannula-decor stickers), then find or make the Indian equivalent. Do **not** import directly at this budget (customs + 30–45 day lead times kill a ₹25k budget).

## 1.4 Where to find them — local (partner's job)
1. **Bhagirath Palace, Chandni Chowk, Delhi** — India's largest wholesale surgical/medical market. Partner should walk it with the Tier A/B list: badge reels, torches, tourniquets, lanyards are all there at below-IndiaMART prices with zero shipping and instant quality inspection. Bring cash, negotiate at 3+ shops per item, collect visiting cards of every decent supplier.
2. **Sadar Bazaar (Delhi)** for generic components: pouches, gift boxes, ribbons, sticker printing.
3. **Local print/3D-print services** for: custom badge-reel inserts, laminated dose cards, 3D-printed name tags (design free in Tinkercad/Fusion 360 personal — Improvement Plan 7.3). Also ask scrub-cap tailors — a local tailor can run 20 caps from your fabric cheaper than IndiaMART MOQs.
4. **Rule:** for identical generic items, local wholesale beats online (no shipping, inspect before paying). Online wins for customized/printed items and when the partner can't travel.

## 1.5 Validation before money leaves your pocket (the "mix" model in practice)
1. **Instagram poll test (free):** post product mockups/supplier photos as Stories on the personal page + Meera page once live — "Would you buy this at ₹249?" polls. 3 days, costs nothing.
2. **Preorder test (₹0 inventory):** list the product on the site marked "Ships in 7–10 days", run it for 2 weeks. If ≥5 orders → buy the 20–30 unit batch (this is your dropship-equivalent without dropship quality risk — supplier ships to partner, partner ships to customer). *Ensure automated WhatsApp and Email confirmations dynamically update their copy to highlight the 7–10 day delivery SLA to keep buyer disputes/chargebacks at zero.*
3. **Batch discipline:** first buy of any SKU = 10–30 units max. Reorder point = when 60% sold. Never more than 3 new SKUs per month.
4. **Kill rule:** if a SKU sells <5 units in 45 days, bundle it into kits at cost and don't reorder.

**Budget allocation of the ₹10k–25k:**
| Item | ₹ |
|---|---|
| Tier A restock + 2–3 Tier B test batches (10–30 pcs each) | 8,000–14,000 |
| Samples from 5–8 suppliers | 800–1,500 |
| Packaging (mailers, thank-you cards, stickers, QR inserts ×200) | 1,500–2,500 |
| Domain postduty.in (unavoidable, from Improvement Plan 2.3) | ~900 |
| Paid-ad test reserve (only after first stranger order; Google credits come first) | 3,000–6,000 |
| Buffer | remainder |

**Prompt for Sonnet (sourcing sheet):**
> Create sourcing_tracker.xlsx in this folder with columns: Product, Tier, Supplier name, Source (IndiaMART/Bhagirath/local print), Contact, MOQ, Price@20pcs, Price@50pcs, Sample cost, Sample ordered (Y/N), Sample verdict, Landed cost/unit, Planned sell price, Gross margin %, Filter score /14, Decision. # PART 3 — Marketing & ads: how to actually get customers

## 3.1 Phase 0 — organic only (launch → first ~30 orders). Spend ₹0 on ads here.
The existing decision stands: **no paid ads until the first stranger order.** At your budget, organic + your unfair advantages (you ARE the customer, you teach this audience, you have two Instagram pages coming) beat underfunded ads every time.

1. **Soft launch to your network** (Improvement Plan 2.5): personal WhatsApp message — not a broadcast — to 30–50 medico friends with the site link and an honest "tell me what's broken" ask. Goal: 10 orders + 5 usable testimonials + bug reports.
2. **PostDuty Instagram content engine — 3 Reels/week, pillars:**
   - *Product-in-action* (15 s): ampule snap one-handed, badge reel yank test, pupil-gauge torch in a dark room. Subtitles always — most users watch muted.
   - *Ward-life relatable* (memes/POV): night-duty humor, intern struggles. This is what gets shared into batch groups — your real distribution.
   - *Behind-the-brand:* packing orders, sourcing trips, "doctor builds a store with AI" — this story is genuinely unusual and both your personal page and this playbook are content for it.
3. **Every parcel is a marketing channel:** insert card with QR → Instagram + "share a story wearing it, tag us, get 10% off next order." UGC compounds.
4. **Testimonials pipeline:** every happy WhatsApp message → (ask permission) → screenshot → Stories highlight "Reviews" + website testimonial strip.

## 3.2 Phase 1 — the parked ₹20k Google Ads credits (after first stranger order)
Google before Meta at your budget, because search intent converts and the credits make it free to learn:
1. Merchant Center feed already live from 2.5 → run **Performance Max for retail** or plain **Shopping ads** on the credits.
2. Separately, one small **Search campaign** on exact/phrase keywords: "ampule opener buy", "badge reel for nurses india", "pen torch for doctors", "gift for medical student india". Low volume, near-zero competition, high intent.
3. Negative keywords: free, DIY, wholesale, bulk.
4. Judge on one metric: **cost per purchase vs contribution/unit** from your unit-economics sheet. Credits exhausted + CPA < contribution → keep spending real money. Otherwise back to organic.

## 3.3 Phase 2 — Meta (Instagram) ads, only at 30+ orders/mo. Read the reality check first.
**Reality check (verified against 2026 India benchmarks):** performance advice online assumes ₹1,500–3,000/day minimums for the algorithm to exit learning phase. You will not spend that. So don't run a "proper" conversion structure — run the small-budget version that works:
1. **Boost your proven organic winner.** Take the Reel that already got the most saves/shares organically and put ₹1,500–2,000 over 5–7 days behind it, objective = traffic to site. You're paying to scale proven creative, not to test.
2. **Concentrate, never trickle:** one campaign, one ad set, 2–3 creatives max, ₹500–700/day for a focused 2–3 week burst (₹8–12k), then stop and analyze. ₹300/day forever teaches you nothing.
3. **Optimize for Add-to-Cart, not Purchase**, until the pixel sees ~50 purchase events/week — below that Meta can't optimize for purchases anyway.
4. **Placements:** Reels-heavy (Reels CPMs run 25–40% below feed). UGC-style creative (customer clips from 3.1's UGC loop) over polished production. Subtitles non-negotiable.
5. **Retargeting is your cheapest win:** custom audiences from site visitors + Instagram engagers + customer phone list; a simple "still thinking about it?" carousel at ₹150–200/day runs profitably even at tiny scale.
6. **Ad compliance guardrail:** never imply clinical benefit or medical claims in ads — accessories language only ("built for night duty", not "improves patient care"). Keeps you clear of Meta's health-products ad friction.

## 3.4 Influencer seeding (cheap, high-fit)
- 10 micro-influencers (5k–50k followers) in the Indian medico meme/study space; DM offering a free kit (~₹300 cost each) for an honest Story — no contracts, no cash.
- Expected: 3–4 actually post; 1 converts followers. Total cost ~₹3k from budget, often outperforms the same money in cold ads at this scale.
- Your own pages count: once the Meera page runs (Improvement Plan Part 3), an occasional organic prop appearance (badge reel visible in frame) is free product placement — keep Meera's page itself non-promotional except a bio link, so its educational credibility stays intact.

## 3.5 Seasonal spikes to plan around
NEET-PG/INI-CET results (gifting), MBBS admissions season (Aug–Oct "Intern Starter Pack"), convocations, Doctors' Day (1 Jul), Nurses' Week (May). Build one bundle + one Reel per spike, two weeks ahead.

# PART 4 — Shipping & fulfillment (order → doorstep)

## 4.1 Channel: Shiprocket Lite (free plan) + India Post as backup
- **Shiprocket Lite (₹0/mo, manual mode)** — already the plan (Improvement Plan 2.7); fine below ~60 shipments/mo. Real cost per shipment: base ~₹26–32/500 g slab, but budget **₹36–50 blended** after GST and zone multipliers. *For items under 100g (lanyards, badge reels, quick-reference cards), package them in lightweight bubble mailer envelopes instead of boxes to access lower-tier courier envelope rates (~₹20–25), doubling profit margins on single items.*
- **India Post Registered/Speed Post** for singles to remote pincodes where couriers surcharge — slower but reaches everywhere and is often cheapest under 200 g.
- **Weight discipline:** every product page lists packed weight; keep every single-item parcel under 500 g so you never leave the base slab. Weigh the packed kit bundles — if a kit crosses 500 g, its price must absorb the second slab.
- **COD: stay off it at launch.** RTO (return-to-origin) on COD in India commonly runs 20–30%, and each RTO costs you both-way freight on a ₹149 item. Revisit only if prepaid conversion clearly stalls, and then with an RTO-risk tool, at 30+ orders/mo.

## 4.2 The fulfillment SOP (print this for the partner)
1. Order lands → Razorpay webhook → order dashboard + WhatsApp + Email notifications fire automatically. Target: **dispatch within 24–48 h.**
2. Partner packs against a **pick checklist**: right SKU/variant → QC glance (torch lights, reel retracts) → bubble wrap → mailer → thank-you insert + QR card → weigh → label.
3. Book on Shiprocket (manual) → AWB number → paste tracking into the order dashboard → customer gets tracking message automatically via native WhatsApp + Email notifications (no Make.com dependency).
4. Delivery confirmation (courier webhook or manual check every 2 days) → triggers the Part 5 post-purchase flow.
5. **Exceptions playbook:** NDR (non-delivery report) → WhatsApp the customer same day to fix address/availability — most NDRs recover with one message. Damaged in transit → replace first, claim later; at your price points goodwill > paperwork.

## 4.3 Packaging that markets itself (₹8–15/parcel budget)
Colored poly mailer or small kraft box, one sticker with logo, thank-you card with: QR → Instagram, "10% off next order" code, and the WhatsApp number ("save us for order updates" = WhatsApp opt-in for Part 5). Source all of it at Sadar Bazaar / IndiaMART in one 200-unit batch (~₹1,500–2,500).

## 4.4 Returns & refunds (aligned with the compliance pages from 2.3)
- Policy: replacement (not refund) for damage/defect with unboxing video within 48 h of delivery; no change-of-mind returns on hygiene-adjacent items (caps); refunds via Razorpay to source within 5–7 days when owed.
- Keep it plain-language on the /refunds page — it doubles as a trust signal at checkout.

# PART 5 — Customer satisfaction → repeat customers

## 5.1 The post-purchase message flow (WhatsApp once live; email until then)
| When | Message | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| T0 (order) | Confirmation + what happens next | Kills anxiety, sets expectations |
| Dispatch | Tracking link | The single most valued message |
| Delivered +1 day | "Reached safely? Any issue, reply here." | Catches problems before public complaints |
| Delivered +4–5 days | Review ask: one-tap rating + "reply with a photo wearing it → 10% code" | WhatsApp review asks convert ~14% vs ~2% on email |
| +60 days no repeat order | Win-back: new-arrivals + small code (₹50 off / free shipping) | Cheapest revenue you'll ever get |

Build this as scheduled native background/edge cron jobs (bypassing Make.com limits entirely to keep operations at ₹0 running cost). Every message needs an opt-out line; utility templates cost ~₹0.115–0.13 each in India — negligible.

## 5.2 Reviews and social proof loop
Photo reviews → (permission) → website product page + Instagram "Reviews" highlight + ad creative bank for Phase 2. Aim: every product page shows ≥3 real reviews within the first two months. A review with a photo earns the customer a code; a Story tag earns a repost — the flywheel from 3.1.

## 5.3 Support standards (one person, sane workload)
- Single support channel: the WhatsApp Business number (also the grievance contact on /contact for compliance). Response SLA: same day, <2 h during evenings.
- **Order-status self-service page** `/orders/[id]` ✅ BUILT — kills 80% of "where is my order" messages. Customers can also look up orders at `/orders` with their Order ID + email/phone.
- Complaint rule: replace/refund fast, ask questions later, screenshot the happy resolution → future social proof. At ₹149–599 AOV, a rescued customer + their batch group is worth more than any single margin.

## 5.4 The numbers to watch (weekly, in your existing dashboard/Telegram digest)
Orders/week · AOV (target ≥₹400 via bundles) · contribution per order after freight+fees · repeat rate at 60 days (target 15–20%+) · NDR/RTO rate · review count · Instagram profile-visit→site-click rate. Track these weekly in the admin dashboard.
